### Chapter 1: The "Tokenization" Breakthrough
The most advanced Suno v5.5 power users are moving beyond "words" and starting to "think" in **Tokens**.

#### 1.1 The "Token" Efficiency Rule
Suno’s context window is limited. Every unnecessary word in your style prompt "steals" focus from the important ones.
- **Bad:** `A really cool and fast song with some heavy metal guitars and a loud singer.` (15 tokens used, 10 are fluff).
- **Elite:** `[Fast] [Heavy Metal] [Aggressive Vocals]`. (3 tokens used, 100% efficiency).
- **The "High-Density" Prompt:** When you use brackets `[ ]`, the AI treats the entire bracketed phrase as a single, higher-weight "Directive Token."

#### 1.2 The "Latent Leakage" Phenomenon
Sometimes, words from your **Lyrics** will "leak" into the **Style**.
- **Example:** If you write lyrics about a "Blue Sky," Suno may subconsciously favor "Major" keys and "Bright" instruments, even if your style prompt says "Dark Metal."
- **How to counter:** If your lyrics are "Happy" but you want "Sad" music, you must over-weight the style: `[Extremely Sad] [Minor Key] [Depressing Atmosphere]`.

### Chapter 2: The "Extension" Masterclass (Correcting Failure)
What do you do when a song "breaks" during an extension?

1. **The "Loop" Glitch:** If the AI repeats the last 5 seconds forever.
   - **Fix:** Clear the lyrics for the next extension and use **only** an `[Outro]` or `[Instrumental]` tag.
2. **The "Vocal Shift" Glitch:** If the voice suddenly changes to a different singer.
   - **Fix:** Use "Remix" on the segment before the change, and explicitly add the Captured Voice ID to the prompt.
3. **The "Mud" Glitch:** If the audio becomes distorted and low-quality.
   - **Fix:** Your style prompt has too many conflicting instructions. Simplify the prompt for the next 60 seconds of the song.
